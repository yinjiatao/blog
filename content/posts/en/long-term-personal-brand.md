## The Anxiety of the Traffic Era

Open social media and everywhere you see:

- "Gain 100,000 followers in 30 days"
- "One video earns $50,000"
- "Make $10,000/month with zero experience"

This kind of content creates anxiety and drives people toward quick fixes.

But a real personal brand needs time to develop.

## What Is Long-Termism

Long-termism is not about being slow. It means:

- **Consistent output**: don't stop because of short-term data fluctuations
- **Quality first**: don't sacrifice quality for quantity
- **Compound thinking**: believe in the power of accumulation
- **Intrinsic motivation**: create for yourself, not for algorithms

## My Personal Brand Journey

### Year One: Unnoticed

- Wrote one article per week
- Average views under 100
- Slow follower growth
- Income nearly zero

### Year Two: Starting to Gain Traction

- Accumulated 50 original pieces
- A few articles started spreading
- Built an email list
- Got first paying subscribers

### Year Three: Qualitative Change

- Content library became a moat
- Passive income exceeded active income
- Opportunities came to me
- Gained the freedom to choose

## Advantages of Long-Termism

### 1. Compound Effect

Every piece of content is an asset:

- Continuously drives traffic
- Builds professional image
- Accumulates trust

### 2. Differentiation

When everyone chases trending topics, going deep in one area makes you stand out.

### 3. Sustainability

Short-term bursts lead to burnout. Long-termism is sustainable.

### 4. Risk Resistance

Algorithms change, platforms fall — but your content and reader relationships persist.

## How to Practice Long-Termism

### 1. Choose the Right Direction

Ask yourself:

- Will I still be doing this in 10 years?
- Would I do it even if it didn't make money?
- Does this direction have long-term value?

### 2. Set Process Goals

Don't just focus on outcome metrics (followers, income). Focus on process metrics:

- How much content did I produce this week?
- How many people did I help?
- How much did my skills improve?

### 3. Build Systems

- Fixed creative time
- Standardized workflow
- Automated publishing

## Closing Thoughts

In this fast-paced era, slowing down is a superpower.

Not all effort has immediate rewards, but no effort is ever wasted.

The best time to plant a tree was ten years ago. The second best time is now.
