## What Is Deep Work

Deep work is professional activity performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that pushes your cognitive capabilities to their limit. This kind of effort creates new value, improves your skill, and is hard to replicate.

## Why Deep Work Matters More Than Ever

In the knowledge economy, two types of people will thrive:

1. **Those who can quickly master complex skills**
2. **Those who produce at an elite level in terms of both quality and speed**

Both abilities require deep work. Without extended concentrated practice, you cannot reach world-class levels in any field.

## My Deep Work Practice

### 1. Build Ritual

From 6am to 9am is my deep work window. During this time I:

- Turn off all notifications
- Put my phone in another room
- Use a website blocker to prevent social media

### 2. Embrace Boredom

Modern people have lost the ability to tolerate boredom. Standing in line, waiting for an elevator, even in the bathroom — we're always on our phones. This habit weakens our capacity to concentrate.

I started deliberately practicing "digital fasting" — setting aside time each day with no electronic devices.

### 3. Set Clear Goals

Before each deep work session, I write down:

- The specific goal for this session
- The estimated completion time
- The success criteria

This clarity helps me enter a flow state more quickly.

## The Rewards of Deep Work

After a year of consistent deep work, I found:

- Significant improvement in output quality
- Greater work satisfaction
- More free time
- Reduced anxiety

Deep work is not just a way of working — it's an attitude toward life. It teaches us to treasure our attention and find inner calm in a noisy world.
