## Why Make the Change

Three years ago, I was a product manager at a tech company. Stable job, decent income — but there was always a restless voice inside me.

I started asking myself: if I keep working like this until retirement, will I regret it?

The answer was yes.

## What Is a Super Individual

A super individual is someone who doesn't rely on a traditional organization and can independently create value. They typically have:

- **Compound skills**: knowledge and abilities across multiple domains
- **Personal brand**: influence in a specific field
- **Business thinking**: the ability to turn skills into income
- **Self-motivation**: ability to work efficiently without external supervision

## My Transition Timeline

### Year One: Exploration

Still working full-time, but starting to:

- Write a blog on weekends
- Learn new skills (design, coding, writing)
- Build a personal website
- Share opinions on social media

### Year Two: Transition

Shifted to part-time consulting, with more time to:

- Develop my own digital products
- Build an email list
- Try different income streams
- Expand my influence

### Year Three: Independence

Fully independent, with income from:

- Paid content subscriptions
- Digital product sales
- Consulting services
- Courses and training

## The Biggest Challenges

### 1. Unstable Income

For the first six months, income was only 30% of my previous salary. Financial preparation and reducing living costs are essential.

### 2. High Self-Discipline Requirements

Without a boss watching, it's easy to slack off. I built strict schedules and work rituals.

### 3. Social Isolation

Working from home can feel lonely. I joined several creator communities and regularly attended offline events.

## Advice for Those Considering the Change

1. **Don't quit cold turkey**: explore your side hustle first to validate feasibility
2. **Save 12 months of living expenses**: give yourself enough time to experiment
3. **Build multiple income streams**: don't put all your eggs in one basket
4. **Keep learning**: markets change fast, stay competitive
5. **Enjoy the process**: the transition itself is growth

Becoming a super individual isn't the destination — it's a journey of continuous evolution.
