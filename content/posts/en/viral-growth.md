## What Is Viral Growth

Viral growth refers to a product growing through users spontaneously sharing and spreading it, rather than relying on paid advertising.

The core formula is:

**viral coefficient = number of new users each user brings**

When this coefficient is greater than 1, the product grows exponentially.

## Types of Viral Growth

### 1. Inherent Virality

The product's functionality itself encourages sharing:

- Dropbox: invite friends to get free storage
- PayPal: referral cash rewards
- Zoom: meetings require multiple participants

### 2. Word-of-Mouth Virality

Users recommend because they love it:

- Apple products
- Notion
- Figma

### 3. Display Virality

Users naturally showcase the product while using it:

- "Sent from iPhone" email signature
- Watermarks on shared content
- Brand logos on free-tier products

## Principles for Designing Viral Products

### 1. Provide Sharing Motivation

Why would users want to share?

- **Altruism**: helping friends solve a problem
- **Self-interest**: getting rewards or recognition
- **Identity**: showcasing taste or achievement

### 2. Reduce Sharing Friction

The sharing path must be simple:

- One-click share
- Pre-written copy
- Multiple channel options

### 3. Create Network Effects

Product value increases as the number of users grows:

- Social platforms (more friends = more useful)
- Collaboration tools (bigger teams = more value)
- Marketplace platforms (buyers and sellers attract each other)

## Common Misconceptions

1. **Forced virality**: sharing prompts that disrupt user experience
2. **Ignoring product quality**: without a great product, growth is just a flash in the pan
3. **Over-optimization**: sacrificing core value for growth
4. **Copying others**: every product's growth strategy should be unique

## The Key to Sustained Growth

Viral growth isn't a one-time effort — it's continuous optimization:

1. **Data tracking**: monitor share rates and conversion rates
2. **A/B testing**: continuously optimize the sharing flow
3. **User interviews**: understand sharing motivations
4. **Community building**: cultivate power users

The best growth happens when your users become your salespeople.
